Portion Control Guide

Portion control prevents overeating & supports healthy weight loss without strict diets or deprivation.

The Perfect Plate Method

Balanced Meal Approach

Fill half your plate with colorful vegetables, one quarter with protein foods, and one quarter with healthy carbohydrates. This simple visual method ensures balanced nutrition.

Vegetables (Half Plate):

Fresh salads, cooked greens, broccoli, carrots, bell peppers

Protein (Quarter Plate):

Grilled chicken, fish, tofu, lentils, beans, paneer

Carbs (Quarter Plate):

Brown rice, whole wheat roti, quinoa, millets

Eat Slowly & Mindfully

Enjoy Your Food Fully

Take time to enjoy your meals without distractions. Eating slowly helps you recognize when you're comfortably full and prevents overeating.

Take Your Time

Spend adequate time on each meal and chew thoroughly

No Distractions

Avoid phones and TV while eating to focus on your food

Listen to Your Body

Pay attention to hunger and fullness signals

Enjoy Flavors

Savor the taste and texture of each bite

Stop Before Feeling Full

Comfortable Satisfaction

Aim to stop eating when you feel satisfied but not completely full. This practice helps prevent discomfort and supports healthy digestion.

How to Practice:

  • Eat until you're no longer hungry, not until you're stuffed
  • Drink water during your meal
  • Wait a few minutes before considering second helpings
  • Focus on how your body feels during the meal

Use Your Hand as Guide

Simple Portion Reference

Your hand provides a convenient and personalized way to estimate portion sizes that are right for your body.

Palm = Protein Portion

Chicken, fish, tofu, paneer

Fist = Vegetable Portion

Cooked vegetables per meal

Cupped Hand = Carb Portion

Rice, grains, lentils

Thumb = Fat Portion

Oil, nuts, seeds

Helpful Eating Habits

Pre-portion Snacks

Serve snacks in small bowls instead of eating from large packages

Serve in Kitchen

Plate your food in the kitchen to avoid over-serving at the table

Stay Hydrated

Drink water before meals to help control appetite

Remember: Portion control is about eating mindfully and stopping when you feel comfortably satisfied.
